Loopin

Overview

Loopin is a mobile app that helps parents of kids with ADHD connect better and build daily routines through simple, fun activities and mood check-ins.

Research and analysis

Through in-depth user research and competitive analysis, I identified gaps in existing mobile solutions and refined my app concept accordingly. I developed detailed personas to guide design decisions and ensure the app meets real user needs effectively. This process laid a strong foundation for a user-centered mobile experience.


Competitive analysis


User Flow

I mapped out a clear user flow to visualize how users navigate the app — from onboarding to completing core tasks. This helped streamline interactions, reduce friction, and ensure an intuitive, goal-oriented experience. Each step was aligned with the persona's needs and motivations.

Wireframe & Mockups

I created low-fidelity wireframes to define the app’s layout and structure, focusing on usability and content hierarchy. These evolved into high-fidelity mockups that captured the final visual design, ensuring consistency in branding, color, and interaction. This iterative process helped translate ideas into a polished, user-friendly interface.

UI Kit

I developed a cohesive UI Kit that includes typography, color palette, buttons, icons, and reusable components. This ensured visual consistency and streamlined the design-to-development process. The kit supports scalability and maintains a unified look across all screens of the mobile app.
